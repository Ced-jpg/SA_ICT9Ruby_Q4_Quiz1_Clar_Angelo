Philippine Landmarks

Landmarks Description

Banaue Rice Terraces, Ifugao

  • Ancient marvel: The Banaue Rice Terraces are an impressive engineering feat, carved by hand over 2,000 years ago by the Ifugao people.

  • UNESCO World Heritage site: Recognized for their cultural and aesthetic value, the terraces have been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site.

  • Iconic beauty: Often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World," the Banaue Rice Terraces offer breathtaking views and exemplify the harmony between humans and nature.

Kayangan Lake, Coron, Palawan

  • Crystal-clear freshwater lake: Kayangan Lake is a stunning freshwater lake known for its crystal-clear waters and underwater rock formations.

  • Iconic viewpoint: The lake offers a postcard-worthy view of the surrounding area and is home to one of the most popular viewpoints in Coron.

  • Part of island-hopping tours: Kayangan Lake is a must-visit destination in Coron and is often included in island-hopping tours.

Mayon Volcano, Albay, Bicol

  • Majestic and Iconic: Mayon Volcano in Albay, Bicol, is a breathtaking sight with its near-perfect cone shape, making it one of the most iconic and recognized volcanoes in the world.

  • Active and Powerful: This volcano is known for its frequent eruptions, making it one of the most active volcanoes in the Philippines.
